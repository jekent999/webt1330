Storyline

Star Wars is about the fight between good and evil, but with an intergalactic twist. George Lucas takes on journeys across a fictional universe where humans, aliens, and droids co-exist. Just like any other great film, much of the success of the Star Wars franchise is due to its story. On the surface, the Star Wars story premise is that of the classic conflict of good versus evil. Within this core shell of a story; however, there are several interwoven themes and subplots.

Skywalker Saga

The Star Wars film series focuses on 3 sets of trilogies, 9 films which are collectively referred to as the “Skywalker Saga”. The Saga was created non-chronologically, with the original trilogy releasing 1977-1983. Followed by the prequel trilogy released 1999-2005, and the sequel trilogy released between 2015-2019

Trilogies

Each of these trilogies focuses on a Force-sensitive member of the Skywalker family and their struggle against the evil Lord Palpatine (Darth Sidious). The original trilogy depicts the heroic development of Luke Skywalker as a Jedi and his sister Leia as they fight against the Evil Galactic Empire. The prequels tell the tragic story of their father, Anakin Skywalker who is corrupted by Darth Sidious and becomes Darth Vader. The sequels features Leia's son, Ben Solo and her and Luke's protegé Rey, 30 years after the fall of the Empire.

Did You Know?

Star Wars is one of the highest-grossing media franchises of all time. Ever since George Lucas first introduced us to Luke, Leia, and Han back in 1977, the Star Wars films have dominated the box office, grossing more than $10 billion worldwide for more than 40 years.
